Time-Critical Logistics: Celebrity Solstice.

Onboard in Singapore during the 12-day dry dock window. DFWIN managed the end-to-end routing, custom logistics, and portside clearance of 2,000 minibar units, achieving 100% on-time delivery.

Logistics & Commissioning

A dry dock is the highest stress event in cruise operations. Our dedicated team is stationed directly at critical ports and shipyards globally, managing container consolidation, customs, and last-mile delivery to coordinate with precise hull construction and outfitting slots.

01 / The Brief

Zero-Tolerance Window

Dry dock is a high-stress sprint. A 12-day window where thousands of contractors refit everything from hulls and engines to cabin carpets. Delays aren't measured in lost goodwill; they are measured in off-hire penalties that exceed $250,000 a day. When RCG brought DFWIN into the Solstice-class refit program, they needed a supplier whose logistics plans were as robust as their engineering.

02 / What We Did

On-Pier Supervision

We put our own people on the ground. We managed consolidation at Shenzhen, handled custom clearances, and monitored the vessels' sailing timeline. In Singapore, DFWIN operations leads were at the pier, supervising the container releases and coordinating with cabin outfitting leads to ensure minibars were unboxed, checked, and positioned in lockstep with the onboard carpenters.

03 / The Result

100% Outfitted

100% of cabins outfitted on time. Zero container delays. Sourcing isn't just about buying; it's about holding the delivery schedule at the pier when the yard gate opens. The Singapore dry dock commissioning demonstrated that DFWIN is as much a logistics partner as an engineering provider.

Why It Matters

"When a cruise ship enters dry dock, the room for error is absolute zero. Having DFWIN representatives at the Sembawang shipyard meant that when custom clearance hit a hitch, we resolved it in minutes, not days. The ship sailed with every cabin fully commissioned."

DFWIN Logistics & Commissioning Lead
